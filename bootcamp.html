11-Week FAANG Interview Bootcamp

Pattern-based, spaced-repetition-driven, 35h/week — delivered fully online. One dedicated group TA per 5 students. One fresh instructor per week. Every Thursday is a mock interview day, run in the same format real FAANG interviews use today.

Why pattern-based

Recognise the shape, apply the template

FAANG problems look infinite. They aren't. ~96% reduce to 20 recurring patterns — once you can name the pattern, you already know the move.

Recognise, don't memorise
20 templates, not 200 solutions
Each pattern is a reusable template. Spot the shape, apply the template — the same 20 moves cover the vast majority of FAANG screens.
Transfer beats volume
Each problem teaches 10 more
Random grinding teaches 200 isolated answers. Pattern-tagged grinding builds 20 transferable strategies that generalise to problems you have never seen.

What each session does · who runs it

Eleven session types repeat across the week. Each has a fixed owner, a fixed goal, and a fixed length — no improvising on the day.

Structure

Daily structure

Every week follows the same 5-day template. Sun–Thu active. Fri–Sat rest.

Instructor · Sun + Mon
5h online · 1 week only
Session + discussion both days, live over Google Meet. Hands off to the next instructor after their week.
Group TAs ×5 · Tue + Wed + Thu
4.5h/week · All 11 weeks
Each TA owns one persistent breakout room of 5 students for all 11 weeks. Peer sprints, peer walkthrough, Thursday mock interviewer.
Lab TAs ×2 · Mon + Tue
3h/week · All 11 weeks
Monday guided lab + Tuesday challenge lab for all 25 students. Hop between breakout rooms, unblock, never re-lecture.
Staffing

24 volunteers

One instructor per week. One group TA per 5 students.

Key recruiting insight: asking someone to teach online for one week (5h live + 2–3h prep, from wherever they live) is a fundamentally different ask from committing for months on-site. Online delivery + the per-week model lets us pull specialist instructors from anywhere in the world — finding 11 one-week experts is easier than finding 3–4 long-commitment ones in one city.
